Bridge is one of the most intellectually rewarding card games in the world. But for many players, the hardest part isn't the play — it's the bidding. Conventions like Stayman, Jacoby Transfers, and Bergen Raises are the vocabulary of modern bridge, and mastering them takes repetition, feedback, and practice.

The problem? There's no good way to practice.

You can read about conventions in books. You can study reference charts online. But when it comes to actually drilling the bidding — sitting down and practicing hand after hand until the responses become second nature — your options are limited. You either need a patient partner, an expensive lesson, or you stumble through live games hoping you remember what 1NT - 2C means.

We built this app because we believe convention practice should be as accessible as the game itself. Here's what makes our approach different:

**Practice, not just reference.** Most bridge resources explain conventions — we let you practice them. You get dealt realistic hands and bid in real time, with immediate feedback on whether your bid was correct and why.

**One convention at a time.** Instead of throwing the entire bidding system at you, we let you focus on a single convention bundle. Practicing Jacoby Transfers? Every hand is designed to exercise that convention. No distractions, no context-switching.

**Instant feedback with teaching.** When you make a mistake, you don't just see the right answer — you see why it's right. What hand pattern triggered it, what your bid communicated to partner, and how the convention logic works.

**Decision Drill mode.** Our signature practice mode puts you right at the decision point. Instead of bidding an entire auction, you're dropped into the moment where the convention applies and asked to make the right call. It's focused, efficient practice that builds pattern recognition fast.

**Multiple bidding systems.** Whether you play Standard American (SAYC) or 2/1 Game Forcing, the app adapts. The same conventions work across systems — only the thresholds and structure details change.

We're bridge players who got tired of the gap between knowing a convention and being able to use it at the table under pressure. This app is our answer to that gap. We hope it helps you bid with more confidence, make fewer mistakes, and enjoy the game even more.
