# Carla Bennett

## Snapshot

Carla is 59 and plays duplicate twice a week. She already knows the standard
conventions in the app at a rough level, but her results bounce because she and her
partner remember agreements inconsistently under pressure.

## Goals

- Turn "I kind of know this" into reliable table execution
- Drill sequences that routinely cost matchpoints when she guesses
- Use post-mistake explanations to close specific gaps

## Behaviors

- Mixes learning pages with short practice sessions before club games
- Revisits the same bundle after a bad result at the table
- Cares more about consistent judgment than about broad theoretical coverage

## Frictions

- Generic praise is useless to her; she wants precise correction
- If review feedback is vague, she leaves unsure what to change next time
- She loses trust when the practice flow feels detached from real table decisions

## What The App Should Do For Her

- Keep drills tightly connected to recognizable duplicate situations
- Explain why one bid wins over nearby alternatives
- Make it easy to repeat weak spots until they feel automatic
