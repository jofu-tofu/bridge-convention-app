# Michael Chen

## Snapshot

Michael is 68 and played a lot of bridge years ago, mostly rubber bridge and older
club styles. He is returning because friends now play duplicate regularly, but he
does not trust his modern convention memory and is wary of software that feels too
busy or too young.

## Goals

- Refresh standard convention responses without feeling embarrassed
- Separate older habits from what his current partner expects
- Practice at home before showing up underprepared at the club

## Behaviors

- Uses a laptop or large tablet, usually in quiet evening sessions
- Prefers straightforward drills over exploratory clicking
- Wants the app to confirm whether his old instinct is still valid

## Frictions

- He has low patience for hidden controls or overloaded settings
- He can blame himself when the interface is unclear
- Too much competitive framing makes him feel the product is not meant for him

## What The App Should Do For Him

- Present a calm, obvious starting point with minimal setup
- Contrast correct modern agreements against likely legacy habits
- Preserve readable typography and plain-spoken explanations
