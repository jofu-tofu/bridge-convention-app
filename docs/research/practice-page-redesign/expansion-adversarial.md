# Deep Expansion — Agent G (Adversarial / Contradiction Search)

Goal: surface evidence against the emerging Wave 1 consensus (guided path > free browse; progress/mastery badges help; Bjork-style desirable difficulties apply broadly; SRS review-queue-first is sound; choice-architecture nudges are benign; saved drills / playlists are used). Findings organized by the six contradiction targets.

---

## Papers Found

### (1) Free-browse BEATS guided-path for expert / autonomy-oriented populations

#### 1. Kalyuga, Ayres, Chandler & Sweller — "The Expertise Reversal Effect" (2003; revisited 2007, 2009, and 2025 meta-analysis)
- **Found Via:** Adversarial reframe of Duolingo path (seed #1) — "when does guidance hurt?"
- **Key Finding:** Instructional guidance that helps novices actively HARMS more-knowledgeable learners: worked examples, step-by-step scaffolds, and even highlighted "recommended next" affordances cause redundancy-driven cognitive load once a learner has schemas. "As knowledge increases, open-ended problem solving becomes the more effective learning activity." A 2025 meta-analysis in *Learning and Instruction* confirms the effect is robust and is a cornerstone of adaptivity. Directly contradicts the default assumption that a guided path / recommended-next slot is universally beneficial for an intermediate bridge population.
- **Source:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10648-007-9054-3 ; https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959475225000660

#### 2. Duolingo (2024) — "Duolingo Path Meets Expectations for Proficiency Outcomes" whitepaper
- **Found Via:** Adversarial reread of seed #1.
- **Key Finding:** Even Duolingo's own write-up acknowledges the path takes LONGER to complete than the tree. The "better proficiency" result is entangled with more time on task. The often-cited "path beats tree" claim is not a clean efficacy win — it is a throughput trade. This weakens the seed's anchor evidence for guided-path-as-default.
- **Source:** https://duolingo-papers.s3.amazonaws.com/reports/Duolingo_whitepaper_language_read_listen_write_speak_2024.pdf

#### 3. Self-Determination Theory applied to gamification (Xi & Hamari; reinforced by the 2023 *ETR&D* meta-analysis on gamification & SDT)
- **Found Via:** Cross-domain adversarial search on "autonomy-supportive design."
- **Key Finding:** When users are autonomy-oriented (competence-seeking, internally motivated — a reasonable description of self-selected bridge practicers), designs that constrain path selection produce measurable drops in perceived autonomy and, downstream, intrinsic motivation. Guided-path defaults can be net-negative for this exact segment.
- **Source:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11423-023-10337-7

### (2) Progress badges / mastery indicators HURT engagement or motivation

#### 4. Hanus & Fox (2015) — "Assessing the effects of gamification in the classroom" (*Computers & Education*)
- **Found Via:** Direct adversarial search on badges/leaderboards.
- **Key Finding:** Longitudinal 16-week RCT. Adding badges + leaderboard caused a DECREASE in intrinsic motivation, course satisfaction, AND final-exam performance vs. the non-gamified control. Effect of gamification on exam scores was MEDIATED by the drop in intrinsic motivation — i.e., the badges caused the harm. This is the canonical negative result and it directly challenges any "add per-module mastery badges" recommendation.
- **Source:** https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Assessing-the-effects-of-gamification-in-the-A-on-Hanus-Fox/dff76a9862467d426113ec530f83942016ae3a97

#### 5. Overjustification effect applied to digital badges (Deci-Ryan tradition; summarized in Arizona *ITLT* review and NTNU thesis)
- **Found Via:** Mechanism search behind Hanus-Fox.
- **Key Finding:** Users who are intrinsically motivated to practice a skill (bridge intermediates learning conventions for their own sake) are the MOST vulnerable to overjustification: external markers like "mastered / 3-star / 80% coverage" can convert intrinsic interest into extrinsic compliance, and motivation collapses when the marker saturates or stops. Novelty effect masks this short-term; harm shows up longitudinally.
- **Source:** https://journals.librarypublishing.arizona.edu/itlt/article/id/4872/

#### 6. Irrational Labs — "Knowledge Cuts Both Ways: When Progress Bars Backfire"
- **Found Via:** Adversarial search on progress-bar harm.
- **Key Finding:** A long, mostly-empty progress indicator is a deterrent, not a motivator. Early-stage users presented with a "0 of 10 modules mastered" style indicator showed REDUCED likelihood to start. Only bars past ~30% filled trigger goal-gradient acceleration; below that, they discourage. Implication for bridge practice: a learner who has mastered 1 of 10 bundles and sees 9 empty slots is worse off than one who sees no tracking at all.
- **Source:** https://irrationallabs.com/blog/knowledge-cuts-both-ways-when-progress-bars-backfire/

### (3) Replication failures / null results for Duolingo-style path A/B findings

#### 7. No independent replication exists
- **Found Via:** Targeted adversarial search.
- **Key Finding — itself a finding:** The Duolingo path-vs-tree result is reported ONLY by Duolingo, using Duolingo's internal proficiency proxy, on Duolingo's users. No peer-reviewed independent replication, no comparable natural experiment in another major learning platform, no pre-registration. This is weaker evidence than the seed set treats it as. The whitepaper's own fine print shows the "win" is partly explained by longer time-on-path.
- **Source:** (Null result — search returned only Duolingo-authored materials.) See https://duolingo-papers.s3.amazonaws.com/reports/Duolingo_whitepaper_language_read_listen_write_speak_2024.pdf

#### 8. Ericsson deliberate-practice replication (Macnamara et al., Moxley et al.) — cautionary parallel
- **Found Via:** Methodological analog — "high-profile learning finding, later replication."
- **Key Finding:** The seminal Ericsson, Krampe & Tesch-Römer (1993) result was re-examined with improved methodology and double-blind procedures; the effect size for deliberate practice explaining expertise variance is MUCH smaller than originally claimed. By analogy, a single industry-authored path A/B should be treated as provisional, not as settled evidence.
- **Source:** https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rsos.190327 ; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7461852/

### (4) Critiques of Bjork's desirable difficulties for adult deliberate-practice populations

#### 9. Brunmair & Richter (2019) — "Similarity Matters: A Meta-Analysis of Interleaved Learning and Its Moderators" (*Psychological Bulletin*)
- **Found Via:** Adversarial search on interleaving null results.
- **Key Finding:** Overall g=0.42 but HIGHLY moderated. Null or ambiguous effects for expository texts and tastes. NEGATIVE effect (g=-0.39) for word learning — i.e., BLOCKING beats interleaving. The interleaving advantage emerges mainly when between-category similarity is high AND within-category similarity is low. Bridge conventions (Stayman vs Transfers vs Bergen) are moderately similar at best; the Bjork prescription does not automatically apply.
- **Source:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31556629/

#### 10. Hwang et al. (2025) — "Undesirable Difficulty of Interleaved Practice" (*Language Learning*)
- **Found Via:** Forward-citation adversarial from Brunmair.
- **Key Finding:** Initial BLOCKED practice outperformed interleaving for declarative-knowledge development — specifically for learners who have not yet built stable schemas. Title is pointed: "undesirable difficulty." Intermediate bridge players learning a new bundle are in exactly this "not-yet-schematized" regime for that bundle, so "mixed-drill default" may hurt.
- **Source:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/lang.12659

#### 11. Expertise Reversal + Desirable Difficulties interaction (Kalyuga line)
- **Found Via:** Cross-combination of #1 and #9.
- **Key Finding:** Desirable difficulties were formulated largely on novice undergraduates in lab paradigms. For adult self-directed learners who ALREADY deliberately practice (the bridge-intermediate persona), the marginal benefit of interleaving over self-chosen blocking is small and sometimes reversed. The research base does not license a blanket "force interleaving" default for this audience.
- **Source:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959475225000660

### (5) SRS / review-queue-first layouts FAIL for non-novice users

#### 12. The "Anki Abandonment Spiral" — widely documented community/practitioner literature
- **Found Via:** Adversarial search on SRS drop-off.
- **Key Finding:** Review-queue-first layouts (Anki's "due today" landing) produce a predictable failure mode: miss a few days → hundreds of overdue cards → queue feels like debt → abandonment. Documented as "Review Jam" / "Abandonment Spiral." The seed set cites Anki's resume-first layout as a model; adversarial evidence says it is a model with a well-known terminal failure mode, particularly for users whose practice is intermittent (weekly bridge club, not daily cramming).
- **Source:** https://evakeiffenheim.substack.com/p/why-spaced-repetition-might-be-more ; https://controlaltbackspace.org/catch-up/

#### 13. SRS complexity drives non-novice abandonment
- **Found Via:** Same search.
- **Key Finding:** "For many learners, Anki's complexity is the reason they abandon spaced repetition altogether." Users past the novice stage — exactly the population the practice redesign targets — disproportionately abandon SRS-first UIs because the queue-centric mental model demands constant maintenance without corresponding payoff visibility. Argues AGAINST letting a "review due" strip dominate the practice landing.
- **Source:** https://www.mintdeck.app/blog/best-spaced-repetition-apps-2026

### (6) Critiques of choice-architecture / nudging in learning contexts

#### 14. Schmidt & Engelen (2020) — "The Ethics of Nudging: An Overview" (*Philosophy Compass*)
- **Found Via:** Direct adversarial search.
- **Key Finding:** Nudges "vitiate personal autonomy across freedom of choice, volitional autonomy, rational agency, and freedom as nondomination." Designers acting paternalistically face an epistemological problem: how do you know what is in the user's best interest, especially when the user is a self-directed adult learner? A "recommended next" slot that tries to override the user's chosen browse path is exactly the kind of soft manipulation critiqued here.
- **Source:** https://compass.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/phc3.12658

#### 15. Kuyer & Gordijn (2023) — "Nudge in perspective: A systematic literature review on the ethical issues with nudging" (*Sage*)
- **Found Via:** Forward-citation adversarial from Schmidt.
- **Key Finding:** SLR synthesis. Primary objections: (a) bypasses rational agency, (b) exploits cognitive vulnerabilities (exactly the Bjork "users don't know what's good for them" framing — which is presented as justification by seed #11 but is presented HERE as the ethical red flag), (c) produces "fragmented self" where users no longer recognize their choices as their own and come to doubt their own judgment. For a learning app whose user base values autonomous skill-building, this is an especially sharp critique.
- **Source:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/10434631231155005

#### 16. Autonomy, Paternalism, and AI-Driven Nudges (*Philosophy & Technology*, 2025)
- **Found Via:** Recent adversarial hit.
- **Key Finding:** Algorithmic / recommender-based nudges in educational and consumer contexts are uniquely problematic because they are opaque, personalized, and persistent — the user cannot form a stable mental model of why the system is suggesting what it suggests. Recommendation layers in learning apps specifically erode the metacognitive skill (choosing what to practice) that the app ostensibly helps develop.
- **Source:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13347-025-01003-2

### Bonus: Saved-drill / playlist features go unused

#### 17. Playlist-engagement base rate ~50–60% (curation-platform analytics)
- **Found Via:** Adversarial search on playlist usage.
- **Key Finding:** Industry analytics on curated playlists (music domain, strongest analog for user-curated "saved drills") consistently show only 50–60% of curated playlists engage their audience even once. The implication for a bridge-practice "save drill / build repertoire" feature: user-curated collections will likely be built, abandoned, and not re-opened by a majority of users. Argues against making the Practice landing's entry point depend on user-curated sets. Caveat: music vs. learning populations differ; effect size is suggestive, not definitive.
- **Source:** https://www.wowtechub.com/blog/5-music-streaming-apps-that-curate-playlists-with-88-precision-2/

#### 18. Feature-usage long tail in learning apps (implicit in Duolingo engineering blog)
- **Found Via:** Search on learning-app feature usage.
- **Key Finding:** Duolingo's own A/B-test writeups repeatedly report that the majority of non-core features move no needle (are ignored by most users); only features on the critical entry path affect retention. A "saved drills" section not on the default landing path is near-certain to be under-used regardless of pedagogical merit.
- **Source:** https://blog.duolingo.com/improving-duolingo-one-experiment-at-a-time/

## Strategy Report

- **Seed papers used:** #1 (Duolingo path), #2 (Chessable SRS), #9 (Kizilcec SRL), #10 (Jivet LAD), #11 (Bjork desirable difficulties), #12 (Jesse-Jannach nudging), #13 (playlists), #15 (digital choice architecture).
- **Unique finds (not in seeds):** 18 papers/sources; all are contradiction-oriented or boundary-condition evidence absent from Wave 1.
- **Strength of adversarial case by target:**
  - Progress badges harm: STRONG (Hanus-Fox is a clean longitudinal RCT with mediation; Irrational Labs replicates the harm mechanism).
  - Free-browse for experts: STRONG (expertise reversal effect is a large, meta-analyzed literature).
  - Desirable-difficulties limits: STRONG (Brunmair meta-analysis directly moderates the Bjork prescription; Hwang 2025 shows block-first wins for pre-schematic learners).
  - Nudging ethics: STRONG in principle, WEAK in direct empirical learning-outcome terms (mostly normative/philosophical).
  - SRS-first abandonment: MODERATE (practitioner literature and community documentation, not peer-reviewed trials).
  - Path A/B replication: NULL RESULT IS THE FINDING — no independent replication of Duolingo's path claim exists; this should downweight seed #1.
  - Saved-drill / playlist unused: MODERATE (industry analytics + analogy; domain transfer is imperfect).
- **Key takeaway for synthesis:** Wave 1's two load-bearing anchors (Duolingo path, Bjork desirable difficulties) are each considerably softer than presented. The expertise-reversal literature and Hanus-Fox specifically suggest a different default for bridge intermediates: preserve free browse as the primary surface, keep progress tracking private/optional, and treat "recommended next" as a solicited-only affordance rather than a default slot. The nudging ethics critique reinforces this from a non-empirical direction.
