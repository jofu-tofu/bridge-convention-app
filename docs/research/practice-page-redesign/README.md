# Practice Page Redesign Research

This folder contains evidence-based research on the Practice tab (convention-select screen) of the bridge bidding practice app: how it should look and behave now that a dedicated logged-in landing page owns resume and marketing, and the Practice tab's primary job is browse/pick with secondary "saved drills" and paywall concerns.

## Files

- `evidence-map.md` — **start here.** Final synthesized evidence map with confidence-rated findings, convergences, tensions, concrete design recommendations keyed to the six design pressures, and a substantive "What This Evidence Cannot Tell You" section.
- `question.md` — the original research question, key concepts, adjacent fields, local context, and the six design pressures to resolve.
- `seed-papers.md` — Wave 1 seed papers (~20) with relevance notes and vocabulary discovered.
- `evaluation.md` — skeptic's evaluation of the full corpus across all waves: tiered paper triage, findings table with confidence, convergences, tensions, gaps, systematic risks, and synthesis implications.
- `landscape-keyword.md`, `landscape-reviews.md`, `landscape-semantic.md` — Wave 1 landscape scans.
- `expansion-backward.md`, `expansion-forward.md`, `expansion-crossdomain.md`, `expansion-adversarial.md` — Wave 2 citation-chaining and cross-domain / adversarial expansions.

## Use This When

- Redesigning the convention-select screen or its nested surfaces (saved drills, paywall handling, recommendation slot).
- Deciding whether to add mastery/progress signals anywhere in the picker flow.
- Designing the "drill preset" concept or planning its extension to the Workshop custom-system builder.
- Deciding how to layer resume, recommendation, and saved content without turning the picker into a dashboard.
- Looking for prior rationale before revisiting defaults around guided-path vs browse, interleaving, or paywall placement.
